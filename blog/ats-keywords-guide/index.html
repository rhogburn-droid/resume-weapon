ATS Keywords: How to Find and Use the Right Resume Keywords

Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

Keywords are the single most important factor in whether your resume passes an applicant tracking system. ATS software scans your resume for specific terms that match the job description — if those terms aren't there, you're filtered out before a recruiter ever sees your name.

This guide shows you exactly how to find the right keywords from any job posting and where to place them in your resume for maximum ATS compatibility.

What Are ATS Keywords?

ATS keywords are the specific words and phrases that applicant tracking systems scan for when evaluating your resume against a job posting. They fall into several categories:

  • Hard skills — technical abilities like "Python," "financial modeling," "Salesforce," "SQL"
  • Soft skills — interpersonal abilities like "leadership," "cross-functional collaboration," "strategic planning"
  • Job titles — "Product Manager," "Sales Director," "Software Engineer"
  • Industry terms — "SaaS," "B2B," "HIPAA compliance," "agile methodology"
  • Tools and platforms — "Tableau," "HubSpot," "Jira," "AWS," "Google Analytics"
  • Certifications — "PMP," "CPA," "AWS Certified," "Six Sigma"
  • Education — "MBA," "Bachelor's in Computer Science," specific university names

How to Extract Keywords from a Job Posting

Every job posting is essentially a keyword map for the ATS. Here's how to read it:

Step 1: Identify Repeated Terms

Words that appear multiple times in the job description are high-priority keywords. If "project management" appears three times, the ATS is almost certainly scanning for it.

Step 2: Separate Must-Haves from Nice-to-Haves

Requirements listed under "Required" carry more ATS weight than those under "Preferred." Focus on required keywords first, then add preferred ones where you genuinely have the experience.

Step 3: Note Exact Phrasing

ATS systems often match exact phrases. If the posting says "cross-functional team leadership," using that exact phrase in your resume will score higher than "led teams across departments" — even though they mean the same thing.

Where to Place Keywords in Your Resume

Placement matters. ATS systems weight keywords differently based on where they appear:

  1. Professional Summary — highest visibility. Include 4-6 top keywords naturally in your opening paragraph.
  2. Skills Section — direct keyword list. Mirror the exact terms from the job posting.
  3. Job Titles — if your actual title is close to the target role, align the language. "Sales Manager" vs. "Regional Sales Manager" matters to ATS.
  4. Bullet Points — weave keywords into achievement descriptions. "Managed Salesforce CRM implementation" is better than "Managed software rollout."
  5. Education — include degree names, school names, and relevant coursework that matches the posting.

Common Keyword Mistakes That Tank Your ATS Score

  • Keyword stuffing — cramming keywords unnaturally. ATS may not penalize this, but recruiters will reject you immediately.
  • Using synonyms instead of exact terms — "managed" instead of "project management" may not match the ATS scan.
  • Hiding keywords in white text — this old trick is detected by modern ATS and flagged as manipulation.
  • Only using acronyms — write "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" to match both forms.
  • Ignoring soft skills — many job seekers focus only on technical keywords and miss soft skill requirements.
  • One-size-fits-all approach — the same keywords don't work for every job. Tailor for each application.

Keyword Optimization by Industry

Different industries prioritize different keyword categories:

  • Technology: Programming languages, frameworks, cloud platforms, methodologies (Agile, Scrum), specific tools
  • Healthcare: Certifications (RN, CNA), compliance terms (HIPAA), EMR systems, patient care terminology
  • Finance: Regulatory knowledge, financial software (Bloomberg, SAP), certifications (CFA, CPA), analysis methods
  • Sales: CRM platforms, sales methodologies (MEDDIC, Challenger), metrics language (ARR, pipeline, quota attainment)
  • Marketing: Platforms (Google Analytics, HubSpot), channels (SEO, PPC, content), metrics (ROI, ROAS, conversion rate)
