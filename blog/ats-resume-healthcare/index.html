Why Healthcare Resumes Need Special Attention

Healthcare ATS systems scan for very specific criteria: clinical certifications, licensure, specialization keywords, compliance terms, and EMR/EHR proficiency. A missing certification keyword can disqualify you instantly — even if you hold the credential but listed it in a format the ATS can't parse.

The Best Format for Healthcare Resumes

Use this structure for maximum ATS compatibility:

1. Contact Information — Name with credentials after it (RN, BSN, MSN, MD), phone, email, city/state, license number.

2. Professional Summary — 3-4 sentences highlighting specialization, years of experience, and key certifications.

3. Licenses & Certifications — Place this HIGH on the resume. Include license numbers, issuing bodies, and expiration dates.

4. Core Clinical Skills — 9-12 keywords matching the job posting.

5. Professional Experience — Reverse chronological, with quantified patient outcomes.

6. Education — Degrees, schools, graduation dates.

Essential Keywords by Healthcare Role

Nursing (RN, LPN, NP)

Patient assessment, care planning, medication administration, IV therapy, wound care, patient education, electronic health records (EHR), Epic, Cerner, vital signs monitoring, infection control, HIPAA compliance, BLS, ACLS, PALS, triage, discharge planning, interdisciplinary collaboration, patient advocacy, clinical documentation.

Physician / PA / NP

Differential diagnosis, treatment planning, clinical decision-making, patient consultations, prescriptive authority, evidence-based medicine, EMR documentation, referral management, quality improvement, peer review, continuing medical education (CME), board certification, DEA license.

Healthcare Administration

Revenue cycle management, HIPAA compliance, CMS regulations, Joint Commission (JCAHO), quality metrics, patient satisfaction (HCAHPS), budgeting, FTE management, process improvement, Lean Six Sigma, population health, value-based care, payer relations, credentialing.

Always list your certifications using both the acronym and full name. Write "Basic Life Support (BLS)" not just "BLS." Many healthcare ATS systems scan for the full term.

Common Healthcare Resume Mistakes

Listing certifications only in a footer. ATS often can't read headers and footers. Put certifications in the main body of the document.

Using tables for skills. Multi-column skill tables break ATS parsing. Use a simple comma-separated list instead.

Missing EMR/EHR keywords. If you're proficient in Epic, Cerner, MEDITECH, or Allscripts, name them explicitly — these are frequently scanned.

No patient metrics. "Managed patient caseload of 25+ daily" or "Maintained 98% patient satisfaction scores" are much stronger than generic duty descriptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What ATS do hospitals use?
Major systems use Workday, iCIMS, Taleo, and HealthcareSource. Smaller facilities use BambooHR or JazzHR. The same ATS formatting rules apply across platforms.
What keywords should a nurse put on a resume?
RN, BSN, BLS, ACLS, patient care, clinical documentation, EHR (Epic, Cerner), patient assessment, medication administration, care coordination, HIPAA, infection control. Match the posting.
Should healthcare resumes include certifications?
Always. Certifications are often hard filters. If the posting requires ACLS and it's not on your resume, you may be auto-rejected. List all current certs with full names and acronyms.
Do healthcare employers use ATS?
Yes — over 90% of hospitals with 100+ employees. HCA, Kaiser, Cleveland Clinic, and most clinics all screen resumes automatically.
Where should certifications go?
High on the resume, right after your summary and before experience. Include license numbers, issuing bodies, and expiration dates.
